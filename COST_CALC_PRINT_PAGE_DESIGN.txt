COST CALCULATOR PRINT PAGE DESIGN

This page will be mostly blank and print a section of information at the very bottom of the page.
Employees will be stapling quarry tickets to the top-center of the page, leaving room for manual notes by hand.
The information display at the bottom of the page will utilize a flex layout system for arranging elements.
I would like a thin border that is top, bottom, and the cell walls in between (no border for left and right sides.)

R=Row, C=Column, fmt=Format

OVERALL LAYOUT: 3 columns.

C0:
	<VendorProduct name> (<Vendor name>: <VendorLocation name>)
	
	-linebreak or gap-
	
	<costPerTon> fmt="$##.## /t"
	<costPerYard> fmt="$##.## /yd"
	
	-linebreak or gap-
	
	(3-column inner layout)
		C0: CHT FSC: 
		C1: <chtFuelSurcharge> fmt="$##.##"
		C2: (<chtFuelSurcharge * tons>) fmt="$##.##"

	(2-column inner layout)
		R0:
			C0: "P: $<productCost>" fmt=currency, 2 decimals
			C1: <productCost * tons> fmt=currency, 2 decimals
		R1:
			C0: "F: $<freightCost>" fmt=currency, 2 decimals
			C1: <freightCost * tons> fmt=currency, 2 decimals
		R2:
			C0: (blank space)
			C1: "+ <chtFuelSurcharge> FSC" fmt=currency, 2 decimals
	
	- linebreak or gap -

	Total: <costPerTon * tons> fmt=currency, 2 decimals (<costPerTon * tons - chtFuelSurcharge> w/o FSC)

C1:
	- linebreak or gap -
	<tons> T
	= <tons / 1.35> yds

C2:
	BILLING
	- linebreak or gap -
	
	(2-column inner layout)
	R0:
		C0: Vendor:
		C1: <productCost * tons> fmt=currency, 2 decimals
	R1:
		C0: CHT:
		C1: <freightCost * tons + chtFuelSurcharge>, fmt=currency, 2 decimals
	R2:
		C0: Total Cost:
		C1: <costPerTon * tons> fmt=currency, 2 decimals
